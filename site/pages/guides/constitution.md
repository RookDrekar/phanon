# The Constitution for the New Coalition of Nations

## Preamble

Be it enacted, by the remnants of the New Coalition of Nations, who have time and time again faced the plagues of apathy, wrath, and the inexorable march of time, that this Constitution be held as the binding document by which we manage, secure, and immortalize our region. Let all subsequent laws and statutes of this region function in concert and accordance with this document in both word and intent.

## Article I - The Architect

- **1.1 Founding Role**  
   - The first Architect shall be the nation that founded the New Coalition of Nations.

- **1.2 Decision-Making Authority**  
   - The Architect has final say on all unresolved matters by the Administration.

- **1.3 Term of Office**  
   - The Architect serves an indefinite term, but may at any time:  
     - Resign their post and grant all responsibilities to the Administration.

- **1.4 Duties**  
   - Guide the region towards prosperity.  
   - Safeguard and curate regional history.  
   - Ensure the continuity of the region.

- **1.5 Succession**  
   - The Architect must appoint a successor if they depart, become inactive, or are unreachable.

## Article II - The Administration

- **2.1 Leadership**  
   - The Administration is led by the **Administrator**, elected by Direct Plurality in the Assembly.  
   - Term: 3 months maximum.  
   - Election Dates: Second Thursday of January, April, July, October.  
   - Assumption of Office: Following Monday after election.  
   - No term limits.

- **2.2 Qualifications for Administrator**  
   - Must be a resident for at least 3 months.

- **2.3 Temporary Appointment**  
   - If the Administrator is absent for 7 days, the Administration appoints a temporary replacement; if they cannot, the Architect appoints one.

- **2.4 Responsibilities of the Administrator**  
   - Maintenance and management of the region.  
   - Updating regional records.  
   - Defense against external threats.  
   - Recruitment and diplomacy.  
   - International representation.  
   - Additional duties as necessary.

- **2.5 Administrative Appointments**  
   - The Administrator can appoint members to the Administration.  
   - Mandated positions: Regional Liaison  
   - Can create or dissolve offices at discretion, except those mandated.  
   - Officers serve at the Administrator's pleasure.

- **2.6 Removal of the Administrator**  
   - Can be removed by 2/3 of the Administration or 2/3 of the Assembly.

## Article III - The Assembly

- **3.1 Composition**  
   - All resident nations, excluding puppets, form the Assembly.

- **3.2 Voting Rights**  
   - Each Assembly member has one vote in elections and referendums.

- **3.3 Leadership**  
   - The Assembly is led by the **Advocate**, elected by Direct Plurality.  
   - Term: Indefinite, requires confidence of the majority.  
   - Removal: Loss of confidence vote by the Assembly.

- **3.4 Advocate's Duties**  
   - Handle disputes within the Assembly.  
   - Communicate Assembly concerns to the Administration.  
   - Provide ethical oversight within governance.

- **3.5 Emergency Powers of the Advocate**  
   - In times of crisis, the Advocate may:  
     - Call emergency sessions of the Assembly.  
     - Issue temporary directives for the immediate safety and welfare of the region, subject to ratification by the Assembly within 72 hours.

## Article IV - General Provisions

- **4.1 Conflict Resolution**  
   - Disputes not resolved by the Advocate may be escalated to an **Assembly Conflict Resolution Committee**, composed of three elected members from the Assembly.

- **4.2 Public Engagement**  
   - The Administration must hold quarterly public forums where citizens can voice concerns or suggestions directly to the leadership.

- **4.3 Transparency and Accountability**  
   - Regular reports from the Administration and the Advocate on activities and decisions shall be made available to all members of the Assembly.

- **4.4 Amendment Process**  
   - Proposals for amendments must be approved by 2/3 of the Assembly.  
   - An amendment must then be ratified by a referendum where it gains a majority vote from all members of the region.
